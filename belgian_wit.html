Belgian Wit

Also known as a Belgian wheat, this beer has a golden color, lacy head, and smooth body!

This is a basic all-grain recipe that I have developed. You are welcome to brew it as long as you give proper credit!

Batch Size: 5 Gallons 4.00 lbs Maris Otter
Original Gravity: 1.042 4.00 lbs Wheat Malt
Final Gravity: 1.010 1.00 lbs Flaked Wheat
SRM: 5 1.00 oz Saaz (5.00%AA)
IBU's: low ~40 0.33 oz Indian Coriander
Total Grain Bill: 9 lbs 1.00 oz Black Pepper
Rice Hulls 1.00 oz Sour Orange Zest
Belgian Wit Ale Yeast (WLP400) 1 teaspoon Bergamot extract
  1. Strike with 180 degree water for 150 degree mash, 75 minutes
  2. Sparge 8 gallons
  3. Boil hops for 60 minutes
  4. Boil coriander, pepper, orange zest for 15 minutes
  5. Chill, measure gravity, pitch yeast
  6. Ferment in primary for 7 days at 64 degrees
  7. Ferment in secondary for 7 days at 64 degrees
  8. At bottling, measure gravity again, boil 1.50 cups of water and 5 oz corn sugar, add sugar water and bergamot extract to bottling tun
